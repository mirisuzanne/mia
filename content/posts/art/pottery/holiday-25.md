## Pay what you want, more or less

When you look around at pottery prices,
the range is dramatic.
I’ve tried to price my pieces
where I think they fit “the market”
in terms of style and refinement --
but it still feels somewhat arbitrary.

I know these prices are higher
than your average Etsy seller.
But I’m not in it for the money.
I’m just happy if I can afford to keep making pots --
and I’m especially happy
when other people are enjoying the things I’ve made.

Ideally,
I’d prefer a pay-what-you-want model,
but my shop setup doesn’t allow that --
and people seem to get stressed out
choosing a price.
Relatable!

Instead I’ve added a
[page of discounts](https://art.miriamsuzanne.com/pay-what-you-can)
and a tip jar.
I encourage you to use them!

- `15-OFF` (15% off your order)
- `30-OFF` (30% off your order)
- `45-OFF` (45% off your order)

Don’t hesitate to bring those prices
into a range that fits your budget.
If you see something you like,
I want you to have it.
That’s more fun for me
than storing it in a cupboard.

Feel free to pass this along to other people
you think might be interested!

I’ve done several commissions, and I enjoy it! Here’s the deal:

1. You tell me what you’re looking for
   (like a wine goblet or a soap dispenser or a tissue box cover, etc).
2. I tell you if that sounds fun.
   The more finishing details (like color)
   you leave to my discretion, the more fun I’ll have.
- If it sounds fun, I’ll try to make it!
- If you like what I make, you can pay what you want for it.

You can ask me for a price,
and I’ll make something up.
But the whole point is to make things that sound fun,
for people who would use them.
So I won’t take the commission
unless I’m excited to try it _for the experience_.

It usually takes at least a month of turnaround time,
depending on the complexity.

because it means something like
_through my fault_,

of how my pottery comes to be.
